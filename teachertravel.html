teachers travel with the council

The Council’s many international connections provide first-hand learning opportunities for North Texas educators. Our previous educator destinations include Morocco, the United Arab Emirates, Jordan, Mexico, India and other places. Each study trip is a teacher’s learning experience that translates into classroom knowledge and inspiration for young minds. Educator trips are provided at a significantly reduced cost for those selected to travel.

upcoming trips

TRAVEL TO THE UNITED ARAB EMIRATES

March 10 - 19, 2017

Educators from North Texas and around the country are invited to apply for an expense-paid March 11-19, 2017, study-trip to the United Arab Emirates in a program supported by the UAE Embassy in Washington, D.C.

As many as a dozen 6-through-12 grade educators from across the nation may be selected for the trip that begins with a one-day get-acquainted workshop at Washington's UAE Embassy on March 10, then moves on to the UAE, a nation of more than 9 million people with a globally respected education system.

"The Portrait of the UAE" study trip includes exploration of culture, education workshops, travel and community events. The program is designed to support curriculum development that "emphasizes 21st century skills and promotes collaboration between American and UAE educators."

Educators who participate in the UAE study trip will be required to present classroom discussions of the experience that can be shared with other districts and at conferences.

The application process is now closed. The results will be announced in January 2017.
