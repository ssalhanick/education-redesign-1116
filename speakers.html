speaker to school programs

Speakers to Schools enhance classroom studies and expose students to international cultures, religions, issues and careers.

$25 WAC Teacher members may request a topic and the World Affairs Council will find a speaker that meets the request. Speakers will visit the high school classroom, speak to students and answer their questions at no charge to the school. This program is incredibly useful to educators in need of relevant resources for their classrooms.

Teachers can choose from 130+ topics that cover subject areas including: the arts, business, conflicts/social issues, countries/regions, education, environment, economy, health/science, history, media, non-governmental efforts, politics/government and religion. Click here for a complete list of topics.

Requirements

  • At least 25 high school students in the audience (No maximum audience size)
  • Students must be prepared to ask the speaker questions
  • At least one teacher per 50 students must be present to supervise
  • Teacher must be a $25 educator member
  • Submit a request at least one (1) month before the proposed presentation date(s)

Please Note: we will not ask our speakers to complete background checks. If this is a requirement for your school, please do not request a speaker.

students to world affairs council programs

Students from local high schools who are associated with an educator member or JWAC are invited to attend our events. We have a limited number of seats for each event. Please see the Guidelines for Attending Council Events for details.

Each JWAC member is eligible to apply for one $100 bus scholarship per academic year.

The best way to learn about which upcoming World Affairs Council programs have education seats available is through the monthly educator news blast. Email Amy Miller if you would like to subscribe to this email list serve.

International Protocol Services

With Beth Huddleston, Dallas Chief of Protocol and Julianna Allen, Manager of Protocol Services
Thursday, January 12, 2017 from 10:30-11:15 AM

Have you ever wondered how the President of the United States avoids embarrassing cultural blunders when visiting with heads of state from around the world? How does he know the proper greeting, what gift to give, how to address the person, and where everyone should sit around the table? Well, it's thanks to the hard work of his Protocol Officers!

In this session we'll explore the world of international protocol services and the comical stories of cultural misunderstandings that happen on the job.

We'll hear from presenters Beth Huddleston, Dallas Chief of Protocol, and Julianna Allen, Manager of Protocol Services for the City of Dallas.
